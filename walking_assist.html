Walking AssiStick

The Walking AssiStick is a device which can be attached to a crutch. It mimics the crutch user's walking rhythm, and will output mechanical acceleration upward generated by the built-in solenoid (an actuator). This additional acceleration will help in lifting the crutch. This project is a demonstration of nonlinear interaction being implemented in human-machine interface.

  • Difficulty:
  • Category: Lab project
  • Completed: September 2019

Entrainment, a type of nonlinear interactions, is defined as a process that leads to temporal coordination of two actors' behavior. In fact, entrainment is a universal phenomenon first discovered by Christiaan Huygens in 1665 when he saw two pendulums attached to the same support become synchronized and swing in opposite direction. Entrainment is frequently observed in Biology. For example, when fireflies come together at night, they tend to synchronize others' flashing rate and time with bioluminescence signal for the purpose of mating. Another example can be found in cardiac natural pacemaker. When individual pacemaker cells are entrained, the pacemaker overall can then output the strongest pulse to initiate a heart beat.

To further investigate the potential application of entrainment outside of Biology, me and my collaborators decided to implement the idea of entrainment at a human-machine interface. The concept generation process led us to the Walking AssiStick product design. We were able to achieve entrainemnt of walking rhythm between user and the Walking AssiStick device.

We believe entrainment would potentially become a powerful algorithm in signal processing besides feedback control and feedforward control. What makes entrainment special is the collaboration element involved between two operators. We are constantly studying the application of entrainment in machine learning and automation.
